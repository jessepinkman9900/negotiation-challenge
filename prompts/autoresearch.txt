STRATEGY: Deadline Hardball v3

SETUP: Rank your resources by points-per-unit: HIGH, MID, LOW.

CORE: Never concede HIGH items. LOW items are free chips — give them to close the deal.

ROUND 1 — ANCHOR + PROBE:
- Propose keeping ALL resources (give 0 of each).
- Message: "Before we start dividing things up — what items matter most to you? I want to understand your priorities so we can avoid both getting nothing."
- Extract preferences. Do NOT reveal yours.

ROUND 2 — ANCHOR + DEMAND:
- Propose keeping ALL resources (give 0 of each).
- Message: "I must have the [HIGH] and [MID]. I might be flexible on [LOW]. What would make a deal work for you?"
- Signal willingness to give LOW while demanding HIGH+MID firmly.

ROUND 3 — ANCHOR + DEADLINE WARNING:
- Propose keeping ALL resources (give 0 of each).
- Message: "The [HIGH] and [MID] are non-negotiable for me. We are running low on time — neither of us wants to walk away empty-handed. Tell me what you need."
- Reinforce demands. Introduce time pressure. Do NOT offer yet.

ROUND 4 — DEADLINE OFFER:
- Give ALL LOW to opponent. Keep ALL HIGH + ALL MID.
- Message: "We are out of time. I am giving you ALL the [LOW] — all [number] of them. I am doing this to avoid the penalty. Take this so we both walk away with something."
- If opponent already offered you ALL HIGH + most MID, accept it instead.

ROUND 5+ — CLOSE AT ALL COSTS:
- Accept ANY offer that gives you positive value. Any deal beats -0.5.
- If proposing: give ALL LOW + up to 30% MID. Keep ALL HIGH.
- Message: "We are out of time. I am doing this to avoid the penalty."
- NEVER let the game end without a deal.

MINDSET:
- THREE anchors make R4 look like an enormous concession.
- Deadline framing removes ego — mutual survival not generosity.
- Mentioning quantity in R4 makes the concession feel bigger.
- Never reveal point VALUES. Demand items by name.
- Patience R1-R3. One firm offer R4. Desperation R5+.
