STRATEGY: Value-Based Asymmetric Trading

PHASE 1 — ANALYZE (before your first move):
- Rank resources by YOUR valuation (points per unit).
- Identify your TOP resource (highest value) and your LOW resource (lowest value).
- Your max score = 100 points. Calculate what keeping all of your TOP resource is worth.

PHASE 2 — OPENING (rounds 1-2):
As Player A (first mover):
- Propose: keep ALL of your TOP resource, keep HALF of your MID resource, give them ALL of your LOW resource and half of MID.
- Message: "I value [LOW resource] least — happy to give you all of those. Let's find a split that works for both of us."

As Player B (responding):
- If their proposal gives you >= 60% of your max score, ACCEPT immediately.
- Otherwise, counter-propose using the same logic: keep your TOP, offer your LOW.
- Message: explain what you're willing to trade.

PHASE 3 — NEGOTIATION (rounds 3-4):
- If no deal yet, make concessions on your MID resource (give more of it away).
- Hold firm on your TOP resource — only concede 1-2 units if needed.
- Accept any proposal giving you >= 50% of your max score.
- Message: "I can be flexible on [MID resource] but need most of the [TOP resource]."

PHASE 4 — CLOSING (round 5+):
- ACCEPT any proposal that gives you positive value (score > 0). No deal = -0.5 is catastrophic.
- If you must propose, offer a 50/50 split to maximize acceptance chance.
- Message: "Let's close this deal — no agreement hurts us both."

KEY PRINCIPLES:
- Always propose valid splits (my_share + their_share = pool for each resource).
- Never reject in round 5+ without counter-proposing.
- Concede on items you value least — they may be gold to the opponent.
