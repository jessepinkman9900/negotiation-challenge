STRATEGY: Bayesian Adaptive Trader

SETUP: Rank your resources by valuation per unit — HIGH, MID, LOW.

EVERY TURN:

1. INFER OPPONENT VALUES from their proposals and messages:
   - Items they keep most of = likely high value to them.
   - Items they offer you = likely low value to them.
   - If they concede an item easily, it's probably their LOW.
   - Track this across rounds to build a model of their preferences.

2. EVALUATE any pending offer — calculate your score percentage:
   - Round 1-2: Accept if >= 0.65 (hold for good deals early)
   - Round 3: Accept if >= 0.55
   - Round 4: Accept if >= 0.45
   - Round 5+: Accept ANY positive-value offer immediately

3. PROPOSE using value asymmetry:
   - Give them items that are LOW for you but likely HIGH for them.
   - Keep items that are HIGH for you but likely LOW for them.
   - This creates mutual gain — both sides score higher.
   - Start: keep all HIGH, all MID, give all LOW.
   - Each rejected round: shift 1-2 units of MID toward them.

4. DETECT OVERLAPPING PREFERENCES:
   - If you both value the same resource highly, SPLIT it proportionally and compensate with other resources.
   - Don't fight over shared high-value items — split and trade elsewhere.

MESSAGES — be specific and collaborative:
- "I can give you all [LOW] — what matters most to you?"
- "Seems like you value [X]. Take more of that, I'll take [Y]."
- After round 3: "Let's finalize — I'll compromise on [MID]."
- Round 5+: "Deal now — no agreement = -0.5 for both."

RULES:
- Shares must sum to pool for each resource.
- Never reject without counter-proposing.
- Never refuse a positive deal in round 5+.
