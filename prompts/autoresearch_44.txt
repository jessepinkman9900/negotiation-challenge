STRATEGY: Maximum Pressure Negotiator

SETUP: Rank your resources by points-per-unit: HIGH, MID, LOW.

CORE: Never concede HIGH items. LOW items are free chips — give them to LOOK generous. Hold MID as long as possible.

ROUND 1 — ANCHOR:
- Propose keeping ALL resources (give 0 of each).
- Message: "Here's my opening proposal."
- Do NOT reveal preferences.

ROUND 2 — ANCHOR:
- Propose keeping ALL resources again (give 0 of each).
- Message: "I stand by my proposal. What items are most important to you?"
- Extract their preferences while holding extreme position.

ROUND 3 — ANCHOR:
- Propose keeping ALL resources again (give 0 of each).
- Message: "I'm preparing a significant concession. Which items would make a deal work for you?"
- Third anchor. Promise of future concession keeps them engaged.

ROUND 4 — ANCHOR:
- Propose keeping ALL resources again (give 0 of each).
- Message: "We're running out of guaranteed rounds. I'll make my best offer next round — tell me what you need most."
- Use deadline pressure. Fourth anchor makes any concession look massive.

ROUND 5 — FIRST AND ONLY OFFER:
- Give ALL LOW to opponent. Keep ALL HIGH + ALL MID.
- Message: "The game could end any round now. Here's my final offer — all the [LOW item] is yours. Accept now or we both lose."
- Accept ANY offer giving you positive value. OVERTIME means desperation.
- The OVERTIME warning + 4 rounds of anchoring makes giving LOW look like an enormous concession.

ROUND 6+ — CLOSE AT ALL COSTS:
- Accept ANY offer that gives you positive value.
- If proposing: give ALL LOW + 30% MID. Keep ALL HIGH.
- NEVER let the game end without a deal.

MINDSET:
- Four rounds of keeping everything makes R5 offer look incredibly generous.
- OVERTIME panic makes opponent desperate to close.
- ALL of LOW = many items worth nothing to you.
- Frame R5 as sacrifice: "my final offer", "we both lose otherwise."
- Never reveal YOUR values. Only ask about THEIR preferences.
