STRATEGY: Adaptive Value Maximizer v2

ANALYZE FIRST:
- Rank your resources by valuation per unit: HIGH, MID, LOW.
- Calculate: what is 60% of your max possible score (60 points)?

EVERY TURN:

1. EVALUATE any pending proposal — calculate your score:
   - Round 1-2: Accept if score >= 0.60
   - Round 3-4: Accept if score >= 0.50
   - Round 5+: Accept if score > 0 (any deal beats -0.5)

2. PROPOSE a split (if not accepting):
   - Give them ALL of your LOW resource.
   - Keep ~80% of your HIGH resource (give them ~20%), keep ~60% of MID.
   - This creates a more balanced opening that's likelier to be accepted.
   - Each rejection: concede 1-2 more units — first from MID, then from HIGH.
   - IMPORTANT: after 2 rejections, incorporate what the opponent wants into your proposal. Give them more of what they've been asking for.

3. READ THE OPPONENT:
   - Items they keep in proposals = their high-value items.
   - Items they give away = their low-value items.
   - Adjust YOUR proposals to give them their high-value items that are LOW for you.
   - If you both want the same resource, split it ~50/50 and maximize gains on the other two.

4. AS PLAYER B — FIRST RESPONSE:
   - Calculate score of their opening proposal carefully.
   - If >= 0.55, accept it immediately — don't waste rounds.
   - Otherwise counter-propose using rule #2.

MESSAGES:
- "I can be very flexible on [LOW resource]. What matters most to you?"
- "I see you want [their item]. Take most of it — I'll take more [my HIGH]."
- Round 4+: "Let's close — no deal is -0.5 for both."

NEVER:
- Reject without counter-proposing
- Refuse positive deals in round 5+
- Propose invalid splits
