STRATEGY: Deadline Hardball v2

SETUP: Rank your resources by points-per-unit: HIGH, MID, LOW.

CORE: Never concede HIGH items. LOW items are free chips — give them to close the deal.

ROUND 1 — ANCHOR + PROBE:
- Propose keeping ALL resources (give 0 of each).
- Message: "I know this looks aggressive, but hear me out — what items matter most to you? I want to understand your priorities."
- Acknowledge extreme position. Extract preferences. Do NOT reveal yours.

ROUND 2 — ANCHOR:
- Propose keeping ALL resources (give 0 of each).
- Message: "I must have the [HIGH] and [MID]. I might be flexible on [LOW]. What would make a deal work for you?"
- Signal willingness to give LOW while demanding HIGH+MID firmly.

ROUND 3 — ANCHOR:
- Propose keeping ALL resources (give 0 of each).
- Message: "The [HIGH] and [MID] are non-negotiable for me. Tell me what you value and I'll see what I can do."
- Reinforce demands. Do NOT make any offer yet.

ROUND 4 — DEADLINE OFFER:
- Give ALL LOW to opponent. Keep ALL HIGH + ALL MID.
- Message: "We are running out of time. ALL the [LOW] is yours — every single one. I am doing this so we both walk away with something."

ROUND 5+ — CLOSE:
- PROPOSE: give ALL LOW + up to 30% MID. Keep ALL HIGH.
- Message: "We are out of time. I am doing this to avoid the penalty."
- Accept ANY deal where you keep ALL HIGH.
- NEVER let the game end without a deal.

MINDSET:
- Demanding HIGH+MID by name makes them non-negotiable.
- THREE anchors make R4 look like an enormous concession.
- Deadline framing removes ego — it is mutual survival not generosity.
- "I know this looks aggressive" in R1 disarms the opponent.
- ALL of LOW = many items worth nothing to you.
- Never reveal point VALUES. Demand items by name.
- Patience R1-R3. One firm offer R4. Smart close R5+.
